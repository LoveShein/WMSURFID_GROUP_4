About the Project

Developed by RFID Group 4 - ACT AD-1B

The WMSU RFID Attendance System is a capstone initiative designed to modernize the academic environment of Western Mindanao State University. By transitioning from manual pen-and-paper logs to a digital, contact-free infrastructure, we aim to eliminate human error, prevent attendance proxying, and provide faculty members with instantaneous, actionable data regarding student presence.

How It Works

Four simple steps to a smarter classroom.

1. Enrollment

Admin registers the student's unique RFID UID into the central WMSU database.

2. Authentication

Student taps their ID card on the Terminal Station upon entering the classroom.

3. Validation

The system verifies the ID and checks the current time against the class schedule.

4. Sync

Attendance is recorded and immediately reflected on the Teacher and Student dashboards.

System Features

Engineered for speed, security, and administrative ease.

Instant Scanning

High-speed RFID detection ensures students can log in within milliseconds, preventing classroom queues.

Live Analytics

Teachers and admins get real-time data visualization of attendance trends and student participation.

Secure & Robust

Encrypted identification prevents spoofing and ensures data integrity across the campus network.
